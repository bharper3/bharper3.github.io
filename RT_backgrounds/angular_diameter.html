Angular Diameter

Angular diameter is a measure of how large something "looks". An object's angular diameter, alpha, is proportional to its linear diameter (size) and inversely proportional to how far away it is.

alpha = 206,265 x (linear diameter/distance)

An object with linear diameter of 2 units and a distance of 40 units has an angular diameter of 10313.3 arcsec.

alpha = 206,265 x (2/40) = 10313.3 arcsec
